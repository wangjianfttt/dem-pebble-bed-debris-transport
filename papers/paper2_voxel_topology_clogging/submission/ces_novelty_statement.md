# CES Novelty And Fit Statement

## One-Sentence Contribution

This manuscript shows that fine-particle breakthrough, sparse-front formation, local debris accumulation and pore-network degradation are separable responses in gas-purged packed beds, and provides a reproducible DEM-derived topology workflow for quantifying that separation in a stiff Li4SiO4 pebble bed.

## General Chemical-Engineering Problem

Gas-purged packed beds can transmit fines to an outlet, or form rare near-outlet fronts, before their connected pore skeleton is measurably degraded. Treating outlet arrival, sparse-front formation, local deposition and pore-network clogging as a single failure sequence can therefore misclassify a transport signal as a structural failure. The manuscript turns this diagnostic ambiguity into a quantifiable transport-and-structure problem.

## Why This Is More Than A Fusion Case Study

The Li4SiO4 breeder bed is used as a demanding, high-consequence packed-bed application with realistic pebble size and stiffness. The transferable part of the paper is the mechanism framework: BTC measures outlet arrival, quantile and tail-fraction diagnostics identify sparse-front separation, debris-volume profiles measure local accumulation, DEM-derived connected-pore metrics measure structural degradation, and the pressure-free Ib index keeps local blockage separate from connectivity loss until pressure-flow evidence is available.

## Evidence Package

- Stiff-particle DEM bed: 10,000 pebbles, 1 mm diameter and 90 GPa Young's modulus.
- Baseline DEM-derived pore reconstruction: porosity 0.4114, largest connected void fraction 0.9921 and box-counting fractal dimension 2.694.
- Transport response: increasing drag-to-weight ratio shifts debris downstream and produces weak breakthrough while the reconstructed outlet-connected pore skeleton remains retained.
- Localized-release response: a high-inventory sparse front approaches the outlet side while the population bulk remains source-retained and no particle crosses the outlet.
- Loading response: injected debris count up to 10,000 raises local sub-voxel blockage but does not create measurable connected-pore loss in the present window.
- Boundary support: cropped-domain pressure checks, claim ledgers, overclaim guard and figure/table provenance files separate supported quantitative claims from proxy or future-calibration claims.
- Parameter-evidence coverage: Supplementary Fig. S25 records the discrete drive-state, loading-state and localized-source support for the mechanism interpretation without converting it into a phase map or full factorial sweep.

## Current Boundary

The present manuscript should be submitted as a bounded DEM mechanism paper, not as an experimental validation, resolved CFD-DEM study, pressure-calibrated safety limit, full factorial parameter sweep, interpolated phase map or universal critical-clogging transition. Chemical Engineering Science is appropriate if the editor values the packed-bed transport mechanism; Chemical Engineering Journal should wait for validation-grade pressure-flow or experimental/CFD evidence.
