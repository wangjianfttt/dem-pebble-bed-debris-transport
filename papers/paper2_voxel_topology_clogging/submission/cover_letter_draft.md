# Cover Letter Draft

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Separating breakthrough from pore-network clogging indicators in gas-driven fine-debris transport through Li4SiO4 pebble beds" for consideration in Chemical Engineering Science.

This manuscript addresses a general diagnostic problem in gas-purged packed beds: fine debris may migrate to the outlet before the pore network becomes structurally or hydraulically clogged. We study this problem in a Li4SiO4 ceramic breeder pebble bed, a high-consequence packed-bed application where debris migration, retention and purge-pathway degradation must be interpreted carefully. The work combines discrete element method calculations with DEM-derived pore reconstruction to distinguish three related but non-equivalent responses: outlet breakthrough, local debris accumulation and topology-level pore-network degradation. The central contribution is therefore not a case-specific blanket-design claim, but a transferable way to separate transport arrival, local deposition and connected-pore degradation in particle-laden packed beds.

The submitted manuscript is organized around three mechanism axes. The drag-to-weight ratio controls downstream penetration and weak breakthrough, localized internal release controls the retained-bulk/sparse-front split, and debris inventory controls local deposition intensity. A high-inventory localized-release case shows that a sparse front can approach the outlet side while the debris bulk remains near the source and no particle crosses the outlet. The response-landscape analysis then shows why these observables should not be collapsed into a single clogging coordinate. A discrete parameter-evidence coverage map further records which drive-state, loading-state and localized-source points support this mechanism interpretation while explicitly avoiding a phase-map or full-factorial-sweep claim.

The main contributions are:

1. A physically stiff 10,000-particle Li4SiO4 pebble-bed DEM model with 1 mm pebbles and a 90 GPa Young's modulus.
2. A DEM-derived pore-structure analysis showing that the baseline bed contains a highly connected pore skeleton, with porosity 0.4114, largest connected void fraction 0.9921 and fractal dimension 2.694.
3. A debris-transport analysis showing that stronger drag-to-weight ratio shifts debris downstream and can produce weak breakthrough while the reconstructed outlet-connected pore skeleton remains retained.
4. A localized-release analysis showing sparse-front formation without coherent bulk breakthrough, supported by quantile and tail-fraction diagnostics.
5. A composite clogging-index framework that separates local blockage, connectivity loss and hydraulic consequence, supported by bounded Ergun, voxel-network and cropped-domain OpenFOAM pressure checks while explicitly avoiding pressure-calibrated safety-limit claims.
6. A parameter-evidence coverage statement that documents the discrete support for the mechanism framework without over-interpreting the available cases as a phase diagram.

We believe the manuscript fits Chemical Engineering Science because it is framed around transport and structure-response mechanisms in a packed granular medium rather than as a case-specific design report. The manuscript does not claim experimental validation, pressure-calibrated safety limits or a universal critical clogging transition. Instead, it provides a reproducible numerical framework for distinguishing fine-debris breakthrough from structural clogging risk, with ceramic breeder pebble beds serving as a demanding application case.

A concise novelty-and-fit statement is included with the submission materials to make the scope explicit: the manuscript should be read as a bounded DEM mechanism paper for packed-bed transport. Pressure-calibrated safety-limit or full performance claims should wait for validation-grade pressure-flow or experimental evidence beyond the present cropped-domain numerical checks.

All figure source data, figure-generation scripts, supplementary tables and supplementary-figure provenance files have been prepared within the project repository, and a lightweight data/code deposit archive has been prepared for public repository upload. The repository DOI/accession, funding statement, author declarations and acknowledgements should be confirmed and inserted before final journal submission.

We confirm that this manuscript is original, has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by another journal. All authors should confirm the final manuscript, competing-interest statement and submission metadata before journal submission.
